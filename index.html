Motion Pictures

  • Descripon: Solo drama event.
  • Duration: Elims: Depending on participation/OTSE Finals: 1-1.5 hours
    • Rules:

    Elims:

  • The parcipants have to perform any monologue, original or otherwise.
  • The time limit is 4 minutes. The parcipants will be asked to stop after they exceed the time limit
  • Judging Criteria: Acng, Characterizaon, overall impact.

    • Finals:

  • 6 parcipants will get through to the final round.
  • Each participant will be given an emotion they need to portray/play.
  • No props will be allowed.
  • All participants will get 30 minutes of prep time.The last 5 minutes of which will be a short rehearsal of the event
  • After this prep me,all the participants will take the stage and will be alloted acting area’s or ‘blocks.’ The actors are not allowed to come out of these areas during their performance.
  • At the beginning the actors will have to stay frozen in a position they choose.
  • The Judge will walk up to a performer of his/her choice and through a bell will signal the performer to start performing. At any point in the performance the judge will, through the ringing of the same bell, signal him to stop. In this case the performer will have to freeze in that position.

  • The performer will have to then continue his piece from where had left off.
  • The duration of each performance should be approximately 4 minutes.
  • Judging criteria: Acting, characterization, command over body (freezing/unfreezing) , portrayal, overall impact.
  • No profanity, vulgarity or obscenity will be allowed.

Block and Tackle

    This event is to test the agility and ease of a person in switching sides. The participants are required to have basic command over English and Hindi languages. There are two rounds in this event- the qualifying round and the final round.

    The participants will be give 10 ice cream sticks each in order to maintain the score.

    Every time the participants will make a mistake (described below), he/she loose an ice cream stick. The person with maximum number of ice cream sticks left at the end wins the event.

    Only First price will be given.

    Round One:
  • Each speaker will speak for two minutes each.
  • The topics will be given on the spot through chit system.
  • The speaker has to speak for one minute in English and one minute in Hindi.
  • The speaker can choose whether he/she wants to speak in Hindi first or English.
  • The time keeper will alert the speaker when he/she has to flip languages.
  • 10s to decide the side, order of language and points before starting.
  • The other participants are required to be vigilant while one person is speaking since any mistakes made by the speaker will only be considered if any of the other participants ‘tap’ and bring it notice.
    • Mistakes include-
    1. use words not from the language chosen,
    2. incorrect word being used (eg- the speaker said pradhmantri for Donald Trump instead of rashtrpati while speaking in Hindi)
    3. Repeating points counts as a mistake. However, paraphrasing doesn’t. The speaker cannot say ‘x’ number of points in English and just translate them in Hindi in the other one minute. However, if the speaker ratifies or paraphrases his/her points in the same language then it is allowed.
  • The person who taps fist will get the first chance to point the mistake. However, the person who points the mistake must know the corrected version or else it will not be counted.
  • If the speaker takes gaps of 5 second or more while speaking then each five second gap will result in loss of an ice cream stick. This has been done so that the speaker isn’t allowed to waste his/her one minute by simply not saying much and thus not committing any errors.
  • In case the speaker doesn’t speak for the entirety of 1 minute in any language, any and all five second intervals will be counted and will result in the loss of those many number of ice cream sticks.
  • To qualify to the next round, the participant must have a minimum of 5 sticks in hand. OC decision is final in any and all matters of dispute or confusion.
  • No electronic devices are allowed.
  • Participants are required to refrain from unruly and foul behaviour, else will be subject to disqualification.
    • Round two:

  • In this round the participants will now have to flip sides along with the language post one minute. Thus the speaker has to speak for a total of two minutes of which- One minute he/she speaks for say Hindi and For the topic, then he/she has to flip and speak in English and Against the topic for the remaining minute. All other rules remain the same.

Survival Event/Who am I?

  • This is a writing event which will test the degree of persuasiveness and creativity in the writing of the participant.(individual event)
  • In this event, the writer will be allotted a character and a specific situation wherein their character is perceived a particular way.

  • They have to write 300-400 words from the point of view of the character such that the reader must be persuaded to see the character in a new light which is completely opposite the one the character is always perceived in.

  • For eg. If the character allotted to them has always been seen as a villain or negative character, then the writer has to persuade the reader to now see the character in a positive light in the same situation.

  • 45 minutes will be given for preparing of the write-up.

  • Write-ups will be judged on-

    1. Persuasion Level
    2. Creativity
    3. Uniqueness
    4. Clarity of thought
    5. Originality of content
    6. Structure of sentences and write-up.
  • Vulgarity, and abusive language must not be used.
  • OC decision is final in any and all matters of dispute or confusion.
  • No electronic devices are allowed.

Fan-Fic

  • The participants must register in teams of 2.
  • This event has two round. This is an unconventional take on a writing event and the participants will be required to form teams such that at least one of them is good at writing.
    • Round 1: Character Auction.
  • Two category of character’s, namely A and B.
  • Category A consists of all comic book heroes and Category B consists of all other characters.
  • Each team will be given an imaginary amount of money using which they will bid and buy one character from each category.
  • The teams will have to write a creative story based on the characters they purchase in the auction. Hence the participants are advised to play wisely.
  • Teams are not allowed to place bids which are greater than the balance they hold.
  • Teams are required to maintain the decorum of the event and are subject to disqualification on the grounds of misbehaviour either with the OC or other teams.
  • The money saved in this round will convert to points @ ___________.
    • Round 2:
  • The team has to now write a story of an interaction between the two characters they have purchased.
  • The teams get 15 mins post the auction to discuss the strategy or storyline with their partner post which either of the following can be done-
    1. Both team members write and then combine their parts into one.
    2. Only one member writes and the other member can leave.
    3. One member can write and the other member can stay but has to sit quietly.
  • 45 minutes writing time will be given to the participants and the minimum word limit is 300.
  • Another character in the auction cannot have an appearance in the story. Passing reference for character building is allowed.

  • The story will be judged on the following criteria-

    • Creativity
    • Contrast of nature of characters
    • Vocabulary
    • Structure
    • Overall effect.
  • Only original work will be accepted.
  • OC decision is final in any and all matters of dispute or confusion.
  • No use of electronic devices is allowed.

Dristikond

  • This is a creative Hindi writing event where participants are required to come in teams of two. This event is a writeup relay.
  • The topic will be disclosed to one member of the team who will start writing. The 1st writer gets to write for 15 minutes.
  • Post this the second writer comes in and the first writer must leave the room, The 2nd writer continues writing for another 15 minutes.
  • Post this again there will be a swap and the 1st writer will come and finish the story. The participant will get only 1 minutes to do so.
  • The time taken to shift between the writers is not included in the writing time.

  • The final writeup will be judged on the following criteria-

    • Flow of the story
    • Creativity and Originality
    • Grammar and vocabulary
  • OC decision is final in any and all matters of dispute or confusion.
  • No electronic devices are allowed.

VakPutata (Just a minute)

  • In this game, every participant is given a random topic to talk on for 60 seconds without any time to prep. It has to be spontaneous. Spontaneity is the name of the game. This is a Hindi event

  • Elements of JAM

    1. Analyze, assimilate ideas
    2. Analyze audience needs
    3. Use proverbs , jokes and anecdotes to retain the audience’s interest
    4. Quote examples from their self experiences.

  • The important rules to be followed are:

    1. No deviation
    2. No repetition
    3. No hesitation
  • Do’s
    • Be ready to speak in an any given situation
    • Be brief and to the point
    • Be cautious of time
    • Follow a sequential order
  • Don’ts
    • Jumble ideas
    • Drag the point
    • Go on and on
    • Give too many pauses
    • Shy away from expressing your ideas
  • Also, every round has a theme, which is usually decided by the Jammaster. For example, one of the most popular themes is from the disconnect round, where every subsequently spoken sentence has to have no relation to the previously spoken one.
  • Finally, objections are permitted by other participants if it is felt that the speaker does not stick to the topic or any such other issue. The final decision of accepting an objection rests with the Jammaster.

Poetry Slam

  • The poetry slam will be a platform for poets to come and share their work. The rules of the slam will be as follows-

    1. Only original work will be entertained. The poems can be about anything; however, they must be submitted beforehand (two days before the event is conducted)
    2. The poets will get a maximum total of 3 minutes to recite their poetry and a grace period of 10 seconds to complete their recitation. Thus, at the end of 3 minutes and 10 seconds, the poem must get over.
    3. No costumes, props or musical instruments will be allowed for the recitation.
    4. The poets can refer to their poems from paper, phones, or laptops while they’re reciting them.
    5. No vulgar-themed poems will be entertained. The OC can disqualify these poems on their discretion. The poet may, however, decide to submit an alternate piece of poetry if a certain poem has been disqualified.

  • The other details of the poetry slam are as follows-

    1. 12 participants will be entertained. No on the spot registrations.
    2. The total time-period of the slam would not exceed 1 hour.
    3. There would be 2 judges for this event.
    4. 1st, 2nd and 3rd prizes would be given.
    5. There would be just 1 round of the slam.

About Us

NMIMS is an educational empire with a 35 year legacy. A multi-disciplinary University with four campuses and 11 schools that include Management, Engineering, Pharmacy, Architecture, Commerce, Business Economics, Science, Law, Aviation, Liberal Arts & Distance Learning.


Today more than 12000 students and 450 full time faculty members are part of India's most sought after academic community. (We did not write the last line, their website did.)


NMIMS School of Law established in the year 2013 approved by the Bar Council of India (yes our degrees are legitimate) aims to provide quality legal education in order to meet the challenges in the field of law.


“We at School of Law inculcate and nourish all the professional virtues in a well calculated and planned manner”, is what the website says. And by “well calculated and planned manner” they mean, a daunting trimester pattern, 80% attendance policy and students who really appreciate extra-curricular activities as a well deserved de-stress mechanism that they can avail of once in a while, amidst the biblical life lesson serving monster that is-School of Law.


We could say Meraki is a cultural extravaganza but in reality, it is a melting pot of dead volunteers’ sacrifices, our committee head’s non-existent social lives, millions of rejected conventional event ideas, a thousand arguments with guards to let us use the classrooms beyond 7pm and all the chilly cheese maggi that was consumed during committee meetings. Meraki 2017 resembles a Karan Johar film-very dramatic, really expensive (ask our sponsorship team), running high on exaggerated emotions and an all out masala entertainer you’d watch at least once. We’ll even play Arijit Singh for a bit.


We’ve made our memes, bribed our volunteers with free food and customised t-shirts, had a long arduous committee meeting where democracy prevailed and each department’s best conspiracy theories were voted for, just for you to participate in and win all our fancy prizes in kind.


There’s a competition for everyone at Meraki, from the dancers to the painters to the singers to the actors and even the gamers. Our goal this August is to give you a platform to shed your inhibitions, show case your talents and give yourself that much needed break from NMIMS.


And no, our theme song isn’t the NMIMS Anthem, we promise.
