Scrap Cloth Fashion Show

“Always wanted to create your innovative attire?

Well, here's your chance, but the items are useless and dire

It’s not about the brand, it’s about the style

Walk down the ramp and slay it with a smile.”

Rules:

  1. Team Composition: 2-3 members, including the Model who will display the designed apparel by walking on a short ramp.
  2. Maximum Teams: 20
  3. Time Limit: 2 hours, all-inclusive except the ramp walk.
  4. The Model must wear a plain black top/shirt and plain black bottoms during the ramp walk.
  5. You will be provided with a basic kit, which will include: black cloth,
        • Black cloth (100cm x 80cm)
        • Scissors
        • Fabric glue
        • Safety pins
        • A4 size paper
        • Pencil
        • Scale (15cm)
        • A stapler  
  6. Participants will not be provided with fashion sense, please try to get your own.
  7. You have to pick scrap cloth from a box kept on the venue and design the most eye-catching apparel using the scrap cloth and basic kit provided.
  8. A ramp walk by the aforementioned model will follow the designing event.

Motion Picture

Solo drama event.  People who are able to act like they are paying attention in an 8 a.m lecture on a Monday morning are urged to participate in this event.

 

Round 1: Elimination

The participants have to perform any monologue, original or otherwise.

Rules:

  1. Individual participation. Maximum participants: 25
  2. Time Limit - 4 minutes.
  3. Judging Criteria:
    • Acting
    • Characterization
    • Overall impact
  4. Six Participants will qualify for Final Round.

Round 2 - Finals

You will be given an emotion you need to portray. Then you will be allotted acting area or ‘block.’ At the beginning, you must stay frozen in a position you choose. Then, The Judge will walk up to you or any other performer and signal to start performing through a bell. At any point in the performance The Judge will signal you to stop, through the same bell and you must freeze in that position.

The Judge could move on to another performer where he might do the same and ‘pause’ the performer halfway through and return to you. You must then continue the piece from where it was left off.

Rules:

  1. No props will be allowed.
  2. You will get 30 minutes of prep time. The last 5 minutes of which will be a short rehearsal.
  3. Time Limit : 4 minutes.
  4. Judging criteria:
    • Acting
    • Characterization
    • Command over body (freezing/unfreezing)
    • Portrayal
    • Overall impact
  5. No extra points will be given to Actors residing in Lokhandwala.
  6. The actors are not allowed to come out of their Blocks during their performance.

Tone Deaf

Band event. You will have to take any song and change the genre of the song to any other distinctly different genre. (For example from pop to jazz)

 

Rules:

  1. Team Composition: 3-6 members. Maximum teams: 15
  2. Time limit: 5 minutes. Amazing, heavenly and/or mind blowing hour long guitar/drum solos will not be condoned.
  3. Teams must submit the name of the song they plan to perform as well as the genre they plan to change it to, at least a week prior to commencement of the event.
  4. A standard Drum set will be provided. Standard Amps will also be provided.
  5. The lyrics of the song must remain the same. No radical change in the lyrics (omission of the chorus, omission of entire verses etc.) will be allowed. Omission of expletives or obscene sentences is permitted.
  6. No vulgarity, obscenity or profanity will be allowed.
  7. Judging Criteria:
      • Quality of performance
      • Adherence to theme
      • Overall impact
  8. Participants are also hereby informed that music puns are plain treble.

Block and Tackle

Are you a silver tongues bilingual master of the universe? Every time you make a Mistake while speaking, you lose an ice cream stick. You will be given 10 ice cream sticks to maintain the score. (Also because this is the only way the OC gets free ice cream) The person with maximum ice cream sticks left wins the event.

Mistakes include-

  • Using words not from the language chosen.
  • Incorrect word being used (For example, the speaker says pradhmantri for Donald Trump instead of rashtrpati while speaking in Hindi)
  • Repeating content verbatim. Paraphrasing, however, is cool.

Rules:

  1. Speaker Time - Two minutes. The speaker has to speak for one minute in English and one minute in Hindi, or vice versa.
  2. Individual Participation. Maximum Participants - ?
  3. Other participants are required to be vigilant while someone is speaking since any mistakes made by the speaker will only be considered if any of the other participants 'tap' and bring it to Referee's notice. The person who taps first will get the first chance to point out the Mistake. Mistake must be corrected by the first-tapper for the speaker to lose a stick.
  4. The will be Round 1 & Round 2. To qualify for Round 2, the participant must have a minimum of 5 sticks left in Round 1.
  5. In Round 2, you must speak in favour of a topic for the first minute, and against the topic in next minute. All other rules are same as Round 1.
  6. Topics for speech will be given on spot via chits.
  7. If the speaker takes gaps of 5 second or more while speaking then each five second gap will result in loss of an ice cream stick.
  8. No electronic devices are allowed.

Who am I?

This is a writing event which will test the degree of persuasiveness and creativity in the writing of the participant.

You will be given a character and a specific situation because of which the character is perceived a particular way. You have to continue the narrative from the character's point of view such that the reader is persuaded to see the character in a new light completely opposite to initial perception.

For example, narrative given is of Joffrey Baratheon burning a gifted book in front of the gift giver. You have to write Joffrey's POV to convey to the reader why Joffrey is not a mean person.

Rules:

  1. Individual Participation. Maximum Participants-?
  2. Word limit - 500 words
  3. Time limit - 45 minutes
  4. Write-ups will be judged on-
    • Persuasion Level
    • Creativity
    • Uniqueness
    • Clarity of thought
    • Originality of content
    • Structure of sentences and write-up.
  5. No electronic devices are allowed.

Fan-Fic

This event has two rounds. This is an unconventional take on a writing event and the participants will be required to form teams such that at least one of them is good at writing. 

Round 1: Character Auction.

There will be two categories that the participants can buy characters from. Category A is selected comic book characters. Category B is other random characters.

You will be given <placeholder amount> Altairian Dollars, using which you must bid and buy at least one character from each category.

Round 2: The Writing

The team must now write a story of an interaction between characters they have purchased.

 

Rules:

  1. Team must comprise of 2 participants. Maximum Teams : <placeholder>
  2. Time Limit : 45 minutes
  3. Minimum Word Limit : 300
  4. The story will be judged on the following criteria:
    • Creativity

Dristikond

This is an unconventional take on a writing event and the participants will be required to form teams such that at least one of them is good at writing.

The theme will be disclosed to First team member who will start writing. The 1st member gets 15 minutes to write.

Then, the Second member comes in and continues writing for another 15 minutes.

Post this, the 1st writer will come and conclude the story. There will be only 1 minute to do so.

Rules:

  1. Language : Hindi in Devanagri or Latin script only
  2. Team must comprise of 2 participants. Maximum Teams : <placeholder>
  3. The time taken to shift between the writers is not included in the writing time.
  4. The final writeup will be judged on the following criteria-
    • Flow of the story
    • Creativity and Originality
    • Grammar and vocabulary
  5. No extra marks will be given for good handwriting, but in exceptional cases the O.C. might give you a tiny golden star.
  6. No electronic devices are allowed.

VakPutata (Just a minute)

This is Just A Minute (JAM). No deviation. No repetition. No hesitation. No taxation without representation. So, don't jumble ideas, or drag the point, or pause too much, or shy away from expressing your ideas.

You will not be given any time to prep. It is purely spontaneous. Therefore, any participant found using time stopping/time-travelling machines will be negatively marked, after a fist bump.

Rules:

  1. Individual Participation. Maximum Participants - <Placeholder>
  2. Speaker Time - 1 minute. Duh.
  3. Every round has a theme, which is decided by the Jam-master. For example disconnect round, where every subsequently spoken sentence must not have any relation to the previously spoken one.
  4. Objections are permitted by other participants if it is felt that the speaker does not stick to the topic or any similar issue. JAM-Master's decision will be final and binding.

About Us

NMIMS is an educational empire with a 35 year legacy. A multi-disciplinary University with four campuses and 11 schools that include Management, Engineering, Pharmacy, Architecture, Commerce, Business Economics, Science, Law, Aviation, Liberal Arts & Distance Learning.


Today more than 12000 students and 450 full time faculty members are part of India's most sought after academic community. (We did not write the last line, their website did.)


NMIMS Kirit P. Mehta School of Law established in the year 2013 approved by the Bar Council of India (yes our degrees are legitimate) aims to provide quality legal education in order to meet the challenges in the field of law.


“We at Kirit P. Mehta School of Law inculcate and nourish all the professional virtues in a well calculated and planned manner”, is what the website says. And by “well calculated and planned manner” they mean, a daunting trimester pattern, 80% attendance policy and students who really appreciate extra-curricular activities as a well deserved de-stress mechanism that they can avail of once in a while, amidst the biblical life lesson serving monster that is-School of Law.


We could say Meraki is a cultural extravaganza but in reality, it is a melting pot of dead volunteers’ sacrifices, our committee head’s non-existent social lives, millions of rejected conventional event ideas, a thousand arguments with guards to let us use the classrooms beyond 7pm and all the chilly cheese maggi that was consumed during committee meetings. Meraki 2017 resembles a Karan Johar film-very dramatic, really expensive (ask our sponsorship team), running high on exaggerated emotions and an all out masala entertainer you’d watch at least once. We’ll even play Arijit Singh for a bit.


We’ve made our memes, bribed our volunteers with free food and customised t-shirts, had a long arduous committee meeting where democracy prevailed and each department’s best conspiracy theories were voted for, just for you to participate in and win all our fancy prizes in kind.


There’s a competition for everyone at Meraki, from the dancers to the painters to the singers to the actors and even the gamers. Our goal this August is to give you a platform to shed your inhibitions, show case your talents and give yourself that much needed break from NMIMS.


And no, our theme song isn’t the NMIMS Anthem, we promise.

Block and Tackle

Are you a silver tongues bilingual master of the universe? Every time you make a Mistake while speaking, you lose an ice cream stick. You will be given 10 ice cream sticks to maintain the score. (Also because this is the only way the OC gets free ice cream) The person with maximum ice cream sticks left wins the event.

Mistakes include-

  • Using words not from the language chosen.
  • Incorrect word being used (For example, the speaker says pradhmantri for Donald Trump instead of rashtrpati while speaking in Hindi)
  • Repeating content verbatim. Paraphrasing, however, is cool.

Rules:

  1. Speaker Time - Two minutes. The speaker has to speak for one minute in English and one minute in Hindi, or vice versa.
  2. Individual Participation. Maximum Participants - ?
  3. Other participants are required to be vigilant while someone is speaking since any mistakes made by the speaker will only be considered if any of the other participants 'tap' and bring it to Referee's notice. The person who taps first will get the first chance to point out the Mistake. Mistake must be corrected by the first-tapper for the speaker to lose a stick.
  4. The will be Round 1 & Round 2. To qualify for Round 2, the participant must have a minimum of 5 sticks left in Round 1.
  5. In Round 2, you must speak in favour of a topic for the first minute, and against the topic in next minute. All other rules are same as Round 1.
  6. Topics for speech will be given on spot via chits.
  7. If the speaker takes gaps of 5 second or more while speaking then each five second gap will result in loss of an ice cream stick.
  8. No electronic devices are allowed.
