Footloose

  • Descripon: Group Dance event.
  • Colleges will have to try and DEPICT A STORY through their dance
  • The number of parcipants can vary from 4(min)-8(max).
  • There will be a me limit of 5 minutes. Any college going above the me limit will face negative marking.
  • Schools must hand in their soundtrack to which they will be dancing, at the CL meet
  • CD/soundtrack provided at the meet will be tested and if it does not work it will be sent back with the list of necessary changes and compability
  • Any CD/soundtrack not given at the meet will not be tested and if it does not work on the final day, the responsibility is not ours.
  • Props are allowed. The teams must inform the organisers regarding the props to be used by them at least a week before the event.
  • No profanity, vulgarity or obscenity will be allowed.
  • Max. teams: 15
  • Judging Criteria: Choreography, Energy, Expressions, Stage ulizaon, Story , Costume

Motion Pictures

  • Descripon: Solo drama event.
  • Duraon:Elims: Depending on participation/OTSE Finals: 1-1.5 hours
    • Rules:

    Elims:

  • The parcipants have to perform any monologue, original or otherwise.
  • The time limit is 4 minutes. The parcipants will be asked to stop after they exceed the time limit
  • Judging Criteria: Acng, Characterizaon, overall impact.

    • Finals:

  • 6 parcipants will get through to the final round.
  • Each participant will be given an emotion they need to portray/play.
  • No props will be allowed.
  • All participants will get 30 minutes of prep time.The last 5 minutes of which will be a short rehearsal of the event
  • After this prep me,all the participants will take the stage and will be alloted acting area’s or ‘blocks.’ The actors are not allowed to come out of these areas during their performance.
  • At the beginning the actors will have to stay frozen in a position they choose.
  • The Judge will walk up to a performer of his/her choice and through a bell will signal the performer to start performing. At any point in the performance the judge will, through the ringing of the same bell, signal him to stop. In this case the performer will have to freeze in that position.
  • The Judge will then move on to another performer where he might do the same and pause the performer halfway through and return to the first performer.
  • The performer will have to then continue his piece from where had left off.
  • The duration of each performance should be approximately 4 minutes.
  • Judging criteria: Acting, characterization, command over body (freezing/unfreezing) , portrayal, overall impact.
  • No profanity, vulgarity or obscenity will be allowed.

Tone Deaf

    Description: Band event.

    Duration: 2.5-3 hours

    Rules:
  • Here the participants will have to take any Rock song and change the genre of the song to any other distinctly different genre. (For example from rock to jazz)
  • Number of participants per team: 3(minimum)-6(maximum)
  • Time limit: 5 minutes
  • Max teams: 15
  • The teams will have to submit the name of the song they plan to perform as well as the genre they plan to change it to, a week before the event.
  • A standard Drum set will be provided. Standard Amps will also be provided.
  • The lyrics of the song have to remain the same. No radical change in the lyrics (omission of the chorus, omission of entire verses etc.) will be allowed. Omission of expletives or obscene sentences will be allowed.
  • No vulgarity, obscenity or profanity will be allowed.
  • Judging Criteria: Quality of performance, Adherence to theme, overall impact.
  • Max teams: 15

Show me the funny

    Duration: 2-2.5 hours

    Description: Improv comedy event

    Rules:
  • This is an improv comedy event with three rounds of three different games which are on the lines of the show “Whose Line is it Anyway?”
  • The three rounds will test the participant’s improvisational skills, wit, humour and how many rickshaws he/she can catch in under an hour. The last one isn’t actually a requirement but if your answer was 1 or more then either you’re a liar or a God.
  • Each registering team should consist of two participants.
  • The rules of the games will be announced at the event. At the end of each round a certain number of teams will be eliminated.
  • Obscenity will not be condoned.
  • Maximum number of teams: 20
  • Judging criteria: Comic timing, Humour, Acting.

Do you even Karaoke?

    Duration: 2-2.5 hours

    Description: Solo singing event

    Rules: Round 1:
  • The first round is a non-karaoke round.
  • Singers can sing any song of their choice. Solo participation only.
  • Hindi or English songs allowed.
  • Duration: 2-4 minutes
  • Participants going over the 4 minute mark will be negatively marked.
  • A backup instrument/ track is allowed. However the track must be submitted to the college at least two weeks before the event.
  • No vulgarity, obscenity or profanity will be allowed.
  • Judging criteria: Quality of singing , Vocal range, selection of song, overall impact.
  • The top 8 highest scoring participants will move on to the next round.
  • Max participants: 20
  • The participants are further requested to choose 2 songs, and email the YouTube URL of the Karaoke version of the songs at least a week before the event.
    • Round 2:
  • The qualifying singers will pick a chit which will decide which one of the two songs they must perform on stage.
  • The karaoke video will then be played and the singer will have to sing along.
  • The time limit for this is a maximum of 6 minutes.
  • Judging criteria: Quality of singing , Vocal range, Musical timing, overall impact.

About Us

NMIMS is an educational empire with a 35 year legacy. A multi-disciplinary University with four campuses and 11 schools that include Management, Engineering, Pharmacy, Architecture, Commerce, Business Economics, Science, Law, Aviation, Liberal Arts & Distance Learning.


Today more than 12000 students and 450 full time faculty members are part of India's most sought after academic community. (We did not write the last line, their website did.)


NMIMS School of Law established in the year 2013 approved by the Bar Council of India (yes our degrees are legitimate) aims to provide quality legal education in order to meet the challenges in the field of law.


“We at School of Law inculcate and nourish all the professional virtues in a well calculated and planned manner”, is what the website says. And by “well calculated and planned manner” they mean, a daunting trimester pattern, 80% attendance policy and students who really appreciate extra-curricular activities as a well deserved de-stress mechanism that they can avail of once in a while, amidst the biblical life lesson serving monster that is-School of Law.


We could say Meraki is a cultural extravaganza but in reality, it is a melting pot of dead volunteers’ sacrifices, our committee head’s non-existent social lives, millions of rejected conventional event ideas, a thousand arguments with guards to let us use the classrooms beyond 7pm and all the chilly cheese maggi that was consumed during committee meetings. Meraki 2017 resembles a Karan Johar film-very dramatic, really expensive (ask our sponsorship team), running high on exaggerated emotions and an all out masala entertainer you’d watch at least once. We’ll even play Arijit Singh for a bit.


We’ve made our memes, bribed our volunteers with free food and customised t-shirts, had a long arduous committee meeting where democracy prevailed and each department’s best conspiracy theories were voted for, just for you to participate in and win all our fancy prizes in kind.


There’s a competition for everyone at Meraki, from the dancers to the painters to the singers to the actors and even the gamers. Our goal this August is to give you a platform to shed your inhibitions, show case your talents and give yourself that much needed break from NMIMS.


And no, our theme song isn’t the NMIMS Anthem, we promise.
